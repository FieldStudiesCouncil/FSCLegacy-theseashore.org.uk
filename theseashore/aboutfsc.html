About the FSC

The Field Studies Council (FSC) is a pioneering educational charity committed to bringing environmental understanding to all.

Established in 1943, the FSC has become internationally respected for its national network of 17 education centres, international outreach training projects, research programmes, information and publication services and wide range of fascinating professional training and leisure courses.We provide informative and enjoyable opportunities for people of all ages and abilities to discover, explore, be inspired by, and understand the natural environment.

The FSC believes the more we know about the environment, the more we can appreciate its needs and protect its diversity and beauty for future generations.

Outdoor Classroom

Each year we welcome over 70,000 people on courses of various lengths to 17 Field Centres across the United Kingdom. Our ability to pass on to them a deeper understanding of the environment and our place in it is absolutely central to the work of the FSC. Clearly it is important to enhance the work teachers and students have done over a longer period and great pains are taken to ensure courses meet the demands of all levels of the main curriculum and specifications for England, Wales and Northern Ireland.

Find out more about the Outdoor Classroom and about Biology fieldwork and Geography fieldwork with the FSC.

You can also browse our database of 16+ modules, including coastal modules for Biology fieldwork and Geography fieldwork

Publications

FSC Publications provide a number of fold-out charts that can be used in the seashore environment. You can order these publications online.

  • The Rocky Shore Name Trail
    Our rocky shores provide a wonderful opportunity to investigate seashore animals and plants in their natural habitat. This guide will help you to identify some of the ones that you are most likely to see. It also describes some of the major environmental factors that influence them. Find out more
    There is also a Welsh-language version available.
  • Common seaweeds
    The seaweeds this key deals with are large plant-like organisms that live on seashores. Seaweeds are usually easily recognisable, this key will help you identify 36 common species. Find out more
  • Lichens of Rocky Shores
    This card is designed to help in the recognition of the commoner species of lichen that are found on a rocky seashore. The lichens depicted grow mainly on hard acid rocks such as sandstone or granite. Find out more
  • Rock Shore Communities - a new type of fold-out chart that helps identify the communities on a rocky shore. It is a guide to the interpretation and explanation of the ecology of the shore. Find out more

Individuals and Families courses

The FSC run a programme of around 600 Individuals and Families and Professional Development Courses on offer at 14 Centres throughout the UK.Our courses are designed for people of all ages, background and abilities and vary in length from a day to a weekend to a week so whatever time you have to spare and whatever your interest we are sure that there will be an FSC course for you.

Find out more about FSC Individuals and Families courses.

A number of courses cover the seashore environment: you might want to look in particular at the courses relating to the rocky shore, seaweed, marine life but we'd encourage you to browse through the courses.
